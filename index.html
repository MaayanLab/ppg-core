Analysis of podocyte transcriptional response following HIV infection
By analyzing gene expression signatures of HIV-infected podocytes in two independent datasets, we uncovered several commonly altered biological pathways, including immune activation, TNF and NF-kB signaling, and a dysregulation of genes associated to the Polycomb-group (PcG) family. Furthermore, we identify a number of candidate drugs which reproducibly reverse the HIV infection gene expression signature, such as Emetine Dihydrochloride Hydrate and QL-XII-47.
BI-2536 as a candidate to reverse diabetic nephropathy signatures
By integrating information across over 20 publicly available GEO transcriptomics datasets, we previously identified BI-2536 as a candidate drug to reverse diabetic nephropathy gene expression signatures. Here we investigate the mechanism of action of this small molecule and find suggestive evidence that it alters a number of key biological pathways, including kidney epithelium development, steroid metabolism and signaling, and inflammation.
