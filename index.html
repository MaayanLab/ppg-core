The images below link to two Jupyter notebooks illustrating the analyses presented at the April 24th PPG meeting by the Mount Sinai Bioinformatics Core. A link to the full presentation is available here.
Analysis of podocyte transcriptional response following HIV infection
By analyzing gene expression signatures of HIV-infected podocytes in two independent datasets, we uncovered several commonly altered biological pathways, including TNF signaling, NF-kB signaling, and a dysregulation of genes associated to the Polycomb-group (PcG) family. Furthermore, we identify a number of candidate drugs which reproducibly reverse the HIV infection gene expression signature.
BI-2536 as a candidate to reverse diabetic nephropathy signatures
By integrating information across over 20 publicly available GEO transcriptomics datasets, we previously identified BI-2536 as a candidate drug to reverse diabetic nephropathy gene expression signatures. In this notebook, we aim to investigate the biological effect of this drug by performing enrichment analysis of the commonly reversed genes. We find that it alters a number of key biological pathways, including kidney epithelium development, steroid metabolism and signaling and inflammation.
